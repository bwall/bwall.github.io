About me

I am a pwngrammer (software engineer and security researcher) with a wide range of experience. Of many topics of research, my botnet research is my most notable research.

I am currently a security researcher and developer at Cylance. I am the creator and author of the blog series "A Study in Bots" on the Cylance Blog which brings deep technical insight into botnets which can assist fellow researchers as well as those defending their own networks against these threats.

I spend as much spare time working on open source projects as I can. Aside from my own projects listed on the right, I have also contributed to a number of other projects. The projects include Metasploit, John the Ripper, Viper, and Wifite.

My Projects

  • HashPump A tool to exploit the hash length extension attack in various hashing algorithms
  • IRCSnapshot Tool to gather information from IRC servers
  • BAMFDetect Identifies and extracts information from bots and other malware
  • PHP Decoder Live tool to decode/deobfuscate PHP payloads
  • ExtractHosts Static extraction of IPs and domain names from arbitrary data
  • fionly Prints out the first instance of a line
  • ssdc Clusters files based on ssdeep
  • fireBwall Modular Windows firewall developed in C# (no longer in development)
